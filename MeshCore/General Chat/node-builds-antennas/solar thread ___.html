A note about Adafruit solar charger
7:21 a.m.
https://www.adafruit.com/product/4755 https://thepihut.com/products/adafruit-universal-usb-dc-solar-lithium-ion-polymer-charger-bq24074
7:26 a.m.
It uses a "not-quite-MPPT" makes it a cheaper solar charger that works quite well. However the side effect of this is a slightly fluctuating voltage reported to the dev board
7:27 a.m.
For many use cases, it wont make much of an impact unless you're trying to track it (such as solar or battery tracking)
7:30 a.m.
From my understanding on the datasheet, the cause seems to be because the BQ24074 is a switch‑mode charger that constantly switches its internal mosfets to regulate the input source
7:35 a.m.
When the input voltage is near the charger’s minimum operating point, the charger will pulse‑width‑modulate the current it draws. The result of which is a fluctuation of the reported voltage by the meshcore board (especially if it samples during a specifically strenuous activity and then idle)
7:39 a.m.
In theory this can be smoothed out using a capacitor. Conveniently Adafruit have pins available for it on the charger board, and they also sell a nice 4700uF one (almost specifically for this)

7:45 a.m.
