hpux735 2025-11-19 11:02 a.m.
My plan is to of course not reinvent the wheel, but I have to be careful where I take inspiration. The washtastic is licensed CC, but non-commercial, and the Solar Mesh isn't licensed at all, so you have to assume it's closed source, even if there are schematics provided. Same with the voltaic enclosures projects. Therefore, I'm left with the Ikoka Stick (nRF52840) and probably LiFePo4werd/Solar1 solar charge and battery management controller. For inspiration. This charge controller, while it only works with LiFePo4, has MPPT with a wide temperature range, supports pack heaters and thermal management, and has undervoltage cut-out. The enclosure will be quite tight, so adding peripherals within it will be a challenge, the connector I'm planning on using for the solar panel has 4-pins, so 2 could be solar DC in and 2 could be 5v Out, but that wouldn't leave any ability to communicate back to the device. I understand that these design decisions won't make it the ultimate repeater board, but one thing can't be all things to all people. I could look at putting an RTC chip on the board if it doesn't impact the BOM cost too much. My hope is to include at least one, and possibly two LiFePo4 cells within the enclosure, and have everything fit within one enclosure linked above
