Companion relay 1.Relays public messages by zero hop repeating any flood messages it has heard 2.Relays Messages with specific companions as destination by zero hop repeating it. 3.Relays messages from specific companions to public flood by normal flood repeating it. 4.Relays messages from specific companions to its next path dest by flood. Use-case 1.Consistency of conversion - By having 1 companion you'll be able to see your entire convo history. you dont need to flip flop between multiple devices to see what's happening. 2.Range extension - By having a relay on the car, you can have it relay messages to your client thats in a much worse position such as in an office building or a store. This is without messing with peoples routings and reducing the airtime compared to if it was just a repeater. Why not Repeater? -Repeaters are meant to be stationary, too many moving repeaters cause extra traffic and messes with routing, while it is predicted to not cause a real issue on message reliability, it can still cause UX issues as message retries and flood to find a new path is timely. Why not Companion? -Companions dont share their messages, so if you have a companion you use daily, then get into your car, now you wont be able to message outside of the car efficiently. if you put a node ontop of your car, now you have to swap your app to your car companion, which breaks up conversations and ruins message history since now you wont be seeing previous messages, DM's now must also swap. i.e if "Jason" in this situation is messaging my client - "Zaos" and sends me a DM, but i actually have my zaos client in my pocket or left it at home since Im inside of my car driving, well now that message could entirely be lost, or i just wont see it since my car client is called "Zaos-Mobile" and its a different ID entirely. It is possible to shared the same key but i have not dived into that and it does not seem ideal security wise. (edited)
