skykingisepic 2026-01-18 12:11 p.m.
@Ed Tap on Contact, tap on Msg, tap on Send (or Channels-Public, tap on Send) and you get a very nice popup keyboard. And you don't need a crappy USB it connects via BLE or WiFi which is redundant anyway since it's stand-alone. This is a Beta release so go to the github repo and create an Issue if you find a problem or have a suggestion. Go to the WiKi where I have created a User Guide to help with navigating the UI. Coming here and proclaiming something is 'horrible' without taking the time to learn how to use it is non-productive. (edited)
skykingisepic 2026-01-18 12:37 p.m.
First, I am really excited with all of the new innovation focused on MC and especially the stand-alone devices (Indicator and T-Deck). Every new T-Deck UI is closed-source sitting on top of open-source MC After over 40 yrs at this, I know how developers feel about their creations and achieving some sort of recognition for their efforts. I have no issue with developers keeping closed-source while in Beta Test to protect their particular UI and workflow until it matures. Once they have achieved a somewhat finished (it's never finished!) product and made a major public release that's given the public a chance to identify the product with the developer, then it might be appropriate to go open-source. At that point, other developers can fork and publish different flavors of the original which will be a copy-cat but hopefully the public will associate the original UI with the original developer. Credit given where credit is due. So I welcome any and all new projects that expand the MC ecosphere, open or closed. There is even another newcomer to add to the mix: https://forge.hackers.town/Wrewdison/Aurora Try them all, pick your favorite, just realize they're all in Beta and things will change quickly (all for the better we hope) (edited)
