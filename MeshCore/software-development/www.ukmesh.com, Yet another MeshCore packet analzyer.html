mrlm 2026-03-16 7:23 a.m.
A repeater-observer is likley to be placed high up with a good antenna, so it can potentially hear everything. Repeaters are busy single duplex devices, they cannot hear when they transmit and they transmit a lot. They may be in a worse position for WiFi/USB connections to send MQTT. There was some discussion of them not reporting to mqtt thier own packets, but this surely just affects their own stats. A compnanion-observer is likley to rely on a nearby repeater and not be as well placed to hear weak packets, but is more convenient to connect. It can potentially hear as much - everything the repeater repeats, more - somethings missed or not reported by a busy repeater, or less - things the repeater chose not to repeat. For a repeater to miss a packet that something else just managed to hear might not happen. If a weak packet came in during or immediatley after a repeat, any companion would be overwhelemed by the signal from the repeater and may need time for the AGC to increase. If it came in before the repeater would probably hear it and stop or corrupt it before it could complete. I think a dedicated or companion-observer would be best if it was well placed such as in a loft. Reception is easier than transmission, it doesn't need the same antenna. There is no question of it being busy and missing something or a repeater not sending everything, it potentially gets two chances. You now detach the firmware and update cycle of the repeater from any restrictions or flaws imposed by an observer. (edited)
