I’ve built a coverage map: https://mapme.sh It shows where you’ve received which repeaters. It works worldwide – not just for HanseMesh. DO1XX - Dirk 2026-01-12 7:19 p.m.
How it works: Your phone connects via Bluetooth to a MeshCore Companion device . The Companion device constantly receives packets that are traveling through the mesh network - messages, adverts, everything. The mapper app listens to this RX stream and combines two things: 1. Your current GPS position (from your phone) 2. What packets you’re receiving (from the Companion) Every few seconds, there’s mesh traffic - someone sending a message, a repeater broadcasting an advert, etc. Each time you receive something, the app logs: “At this GPS location, I received a signal.” The path tells us which repeater: Every packet that travels through the mesh has a path - the list of repeaters it hopped through. So if you receive a packet with path ['a1', 'b2'], you know: ∙ You have coverage from repeater b2 (the last hop) ∙ The signal traveled: origin → repeater a1 → repeater b2 → you The main purpose: Building a coverage map - showing where you have reception and where you don’t. Green hexes = signal received. Gray hexes = visited but no signal. Over time, as many people map different areas, we get a complete picture of the mesh network’s coverage. No active transmitting needed - you’re just passively listening to mesh traffic and logging where you received it.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​ If you’re in an area with no mesh activity, there’s nothing to listen to. In that case, you can switch to TX mode - the mapper will actively send packets. If a repeater receives your packet and responds, you’ve confirmed coverage. This is useful for mapping remote areas, but generates traffic, so passive RX mode is preferred when possible.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
7:21 p.m.
The app also collects all adverts it hears. Repeaters broadcast advertisements containing their name, type, and GPS coordinates. By gathering these adverts from all mappers, we automatically build a map of all repeater locations - no manual entry needed.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
