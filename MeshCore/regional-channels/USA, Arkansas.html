ThatGuy 2026-01-06 5:13 p.m.
Inside the attic probably isn't going to work well. Most of these devices are transmitting at some fraction of a single watt of power. Even foliage degrades the signal, particularly if it's wet. It's just a function of this part of the spectrum. Line of sight is critical. You'll lose significant amounts of signal if you're shooting through insulation board, roof decking, shingles/tiles and then to the outside world. Suggest you try mounting it outside, either under an eave up high with the antenna sticking out, or if in a weatherproof enclosure, up in a tree or up a pole. Also, invest in a good antenna. Hope this helps. (edited)

Robert - N5QM 2026-01-26 4:42 p.m.
I know it doesn't generally come up in conversation, but I ask you guys to consider the maintainability of each system. There is where I, as an infrastructure operator, see distinct differences. Each area is different, but specifically I am looking to operate solar powered infrastructure with little to no external dependancies such that it can be used when there is no internet or electricity. Historically if you have a tower mounted NRF device on MT, that means climbing the tower to upgrade firmware or risk bricking the device via a bluetooth OTA upgrade. On the MC side, there is a bootloader release that not only prevents bricking the device by restarting in the bluetooth OTA(DFU) mode if the OTA fails, but also makes some improvements on the speed of the bluetooth transfer. On the ESP32 side with MC you can do an OTA by remotely triggering integrated WIFI system to activate in AP mode and then connecting to that AP and uploading the new firmware. MT is catching up on this as they are actively working on the OTA process for the ESP. That being said, there is little advantage to running a power hungry ESP device as an infrastructure node in comparison to the efficiency of the NRF based devices.
4:42 p.m.
Sorry for the wall of text, but I wanted to share a perspective rarely taken into account.

Robert - N5QM 2026-01-26 7:54 p.m.
That is a difficult question to answer. From a technical perspective I "prefer" MC because of the routing and maintainability of the infrastructure. I think MT will catch up eventually on the technical side of things, but trying to make significant changes while maintaining backwards compatability is going to be a difficult challenge that will likely slow down progress. That being said, overcoming the head start that MT has in regards to the number of users is likely going to be the biggest challenge for MC.
7:56 p.m.
The reality is they are great at different things and others have come to the same conclustion based on my research. If you are camping or doing search and rescue type of activities, MT is the way to go as each device can also act as a repeater. However, if your challenge is to cover a large geographic area, I expect that MC is the best way to go.
7:56 p.m.
Of course, these are just my opinions and others may have different preferences or priorities making their situation different.
