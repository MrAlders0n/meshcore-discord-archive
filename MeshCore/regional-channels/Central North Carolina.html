Mizman102 2026-03-29 10:26 a.m.
Have y'all seen the Github projects by Mintylinux regarding Wardriving? It allows users to drive around with their already configured companion devices (no change of firmware needed, it's immediate plug and play) and collect signal strength and location data, which can then be uploaded to a detailed community driven map showing what areas can receive signal from what repeaters! It's a great way to visualize what areas are dead zones in the mesh, making it incredibly easy to determine the most beneficial places to set up new repeaters. The author of this software also provides the map source code along with instructions on how to set up your own (private) map as a backup, to not solely rely on the community map, as the app allows you to upload to multiple map hosts. Once I get my GAT562 30s Kit in the mail, I can't wait to map out our local area to see where we can improve our mesh!
