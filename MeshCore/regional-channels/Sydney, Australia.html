pacjunk 2025-10-06 1:52 a.m.
As I've mentioned before, you cant really look at SNR figures as the range of possible SNRs change with spreading factor. Lowest reliable SNR for SF10 is -15. Lowest for SF7 is -7.5. https://www.thethingsnetwork.org/docs/lorawan/rssi-and-snr/snr-demodulation-limits.png On SF10 you won't see more than about +6, but with SF7, you will see much higher. The only way to really tell is to try it - which you have done. If SF7/8 improves the link reliability, then this is all that matters really. Given some of the experience in Sydney where they found SF11 was better, they may find SF8 is better than SF7. Only way to tell is to try it.

Wide bandwidth is supposed to help with interference as it occupies a smaller part of the "chirp". Dodging the interference with narrow BW is great until something pops up on your frequency. Anyway the truth is in the pudding, and a lot have found narrow to work better. CR doesn't increase sensitivity, it just adds more error correction (and time).
