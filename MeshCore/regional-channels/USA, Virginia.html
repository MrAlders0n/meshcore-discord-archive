UP 2025-09-14 8:46 p.m.
LoRa tends to be rather much line of sight in my experience. From my home without a good placement I was getting about 300m range. My friend had the expensive gear, but lived in a geographic hole for a bit, and just couldn't get signals in or out at all. Getting a node located on top of a big hill really opens everything up, even with the most rudamentry equipment, based on my experience with meshtastic. Making sure to get actual dipoles for antenna helps, but given that it's on a rooftop mast, you could add a ground plane to compensate if you're working with one that's not. I'm in Georgia, but have aspirations to help get the southeast linked up. Good luck!
