I think what I was trying to convey got misunderstood.
  • There are almost certainly other use cases where having a eastern states region would be useful, and we probably should have one. What I think would be harmful is a long list of similar yet distinct regions named for specific use cases.
  • Something like a "us-eastern" region could be used by an AT hikers channel, and also used for a channel sharing hurricane information across states, as well as many other things that have nothing to do with the trail. My argument isn't against a several state region, but that more thought be put into making regions general purpose, rather than naming one for each use case.
  • Another concern I had reading the suggestion a first was that lines of sight around the trail are wildly variable, and routes are likely to need to leave the trail area, especially over longer distances.
  • Does anyone here know of already existing threads or groups dedicated to national-scale coordination, like what larger-than-state regions should be called and how to tailor them to be the most useful to everyone?
