UP 2025-11-07 2:04 p.m.
Update: Droopy's battery seems to have given out, and I'm rather concerned about the 500kHz bandwidth requirement we've blissfully overlooked. In the meanwhile, however, I did have an idea that could be helpful: I will be setting Coding Rate to 8 on any LoRa deployments going forward. Unlike frequency, Bandwidth, and Spreading Factor, which fundamentally break compatibility if any of them don't match, Coding rate is the level of error correction used to transmit, and devices with different coding rates can talk to each other. My rationale is that making a breaking change in an existing network is always difficult, and as meshes form and grow, the concern starts out being whether we can reach each other, and later becomes congestion. Right now, with very few meshcore neighbors, we need to reach farther, but later, it will become difficult to change breaking parameters, so I think that right now, regardless of what other parameters are determined to address the regulatory issues, we should max out the nonbreaking one until density arrives.
